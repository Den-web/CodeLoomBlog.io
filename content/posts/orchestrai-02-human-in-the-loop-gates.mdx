“The model said it — order created” sounds great on a pitch deck. For e-commerce with real money it is an **anti-pattern**. Here is the contour we use in **OrchestrAI**.

[Part 1](/posts/orchestrai-01-ui-ai-vs-server-ai) — UI AI vs Server AI.

---

## LLM ≠ contract

Models are probabilistic: slot hallucinations, wording drift, retries. The product must **survive** a bad turn without corrupting state.

---

## Theory: the contour

```
intent → structured draft → missing fields → confirm → execute → audit
```

- **Confidence** and **blockers** at every step
- Explicit **gates** before a final `Order`
- The operator is part of the **state machine**, not decoration

Server AI from part 1 **never** bypasses review gates for money-moving actions.

---

## In our stack (high level)

- DM → `AIExtraction` / order state → **draft gate** (confidence, slots, catalog, blockers)
- **Review queue** — operator edits and confirms
- **AuditLog** — trace from message to order

We do **not**:

- Auto-send “final” text to the customer without a gate
- Create `Order` below threshold without override + audit
- Hide blockers for prettier UX
