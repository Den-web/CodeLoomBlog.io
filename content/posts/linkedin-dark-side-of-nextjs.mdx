## Thesis

Next.js is excellent default advice for many greenfield apps — and a **costly mismatch** when your constraints are wrong for the framework’s opinions.

---

## When Next.js fights you

| Signal                                    | Why it hurts                           |
| ----------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Heavy **SPA** with minimal SEO            | App Router ceremony without payoff     |
| **Multi-tenant** edge rules everywhere    | Middleware complexity + debugging cost |
| **Legacy build** (custom webpack plugins) | Turbopack / upgrade tax                |
| Team has **no** React depth               | Framework churn becomes the product    |

---

## Anti-patterns I see in the wild

- Choosing Next **because Vercel** — not because rendering model fits
- stuffing **admin dashboards** into RSC before you need streaming SEO
- ignoring **caching semantics** and calling the framework “buggy”

---

## Better questions than “Next or not?”

1. What is the **rendering** requirement in 12 months?
2. Where does **auth** live — edge, Node, external IdP?
3. Who maintains **infra** — serverless-only or hybrid?
