## Hook (what I posted)

“Something scary happened again in frontend” — meaning another **dense** Next.js release, not drama for clicks. v15.5 is the kind of update you skim once and regret ignoring in two sprints.

---

## Turbopack

- Faster dev feedback loops for large monorepos
- Still: verify **custom webpack loaders** and exotic Babel plugins before flipping the team flag
- Keep a **fallback script** (`next dev --webpack`) in CI docs for one release cycle

---

## App Router & caching

- Re-read **default caching** for `fetch` and Route Handlers if you upgraded from 14.x recently
- `experimental` flags move — pin versions in `package.json` and document who owns the upgrade PR

---

## Migration checklist (short)

1. `npx @next/codemod@latest` where applicable
2. Run **E2E smoke** on auth + middleware routes
3. Compare **cold build** time CI before/after Turbopack
4. Post one **internal note** with “we enable / we wait” per feature flag

## Takeaway

Changelog posts earn reach when they answer: **“Do I need to act this week?”** — not when they paste the whole release notes.
